1. Stable Radio Channel: SINR, RSRP, and CQI are consistently within expected ranges. MCS selections remain high and stable, indicating clean spectrum without interference. BLER is near zero, showing reliable link quality.
2. Balanced Resource Utilization: PRBs are allocated proportionally to active UE demand. Neither over-provisioning (wasted capacity) nor under-provisioning (user starvation) is observed. Throughput tracks closely with PRB allocation.
3. Healthy Compute Execution: RAN threads — including PDCP worker and MAC scheduler — complete their execution within expected runtime bounds. CPU utilization per core is moderate with no contention spikes. Thread scheduling latency histograms show narrow, well-centered distributions.
4. Clean Fronthaul Transport: Fronthaul link between O-RU and O-DU shows minimal packet loss, low latency, and stable bandwidth utilization. eCPRI frame delivery is consistent with no signs of congestion or drops.
5. External RF Interference Signature: SINR drops sharply across multiple UEs simultaneously, while platform KPIs remain unaffected. The interference pattern is symmetric across UEs — not isolated to one — indicating an external source rather than a per-UE channel fade.
6. Forced Modulation Downgrade: MCS index drops abruptly as the gNB adapts to degraded channel conditions. CQI reports from UEs fall, triggering the scheduler to select lower-order modulation (QPSK instead of 64QAM), reducing spectral efficiency.
7. Reactive Throughput Collapse: Throughput decreases as a direct consequence of SINR degradation and MCS downgrade. The collapse is correlated with radio layer signals, not platform or network metrics, isolating the root cause to the PHY layer.
8. Error Rate Spike Under Interference: BLER increases significantly during the interference window. The block error pattern is simultaneous across UEs rather than sporadic, consistent with broadband interference from a co-channel USRP source rather than localized fading.
9. PDCP Processing Overload: The pdcp_worker_0 thread runtime histogram shifts to significantly higher values. Tail latencies for PDCP processing increase, indicating CPU starvation caused by external stress-ng competition on the same core. Radio KPIs may initially appear normal but degrade as the packet pipeline backs up.
10. MAC Scheduler Delay Accumulation: The TxCtrl scheduler thread exhibits elevated runtime and scheduling latency. This manifests as irregular PRB allocation timing — not steady per-slot scheduling — causing bursty throughput even under stable radio conditions. The symptom appears in platform KPIs before propagating to radio KPIs.
11. Thread Affinity Misconfiguration: RAN threads are observed running on non-designated CPU cores (detectable through per-core utilization patterns). This causes cache miss penalties and memory access latency, producing elevated and erratic thread execution times without any external load being applied.
12. Platform-Radio Decoupling: Radio KPIs (SINR, BLER, CQI) remain stable while platform KPIs (CPU utilization, thread runtime) are anomalous. This decoupling pattern is a distinctive signature of platform-layer anomalies, distinguishing them from radio or network causes.
13. Fronthaul Link Saturation: Network KPIs on the DU-RU port show elevated utilization, increased packet loss, or increased round-trip latency. The FH link is operating at or near capacity, preventing timely delivery of I/Q samples and control messages between O-RU and O-DU.
14. Fronthaul-Induced PHY Degradation: Radio KPIs degrade (BLER increases, throughput drops) as a downstream effect of FH link congestion, but platform KPIs remain healthy. This distinguishes FH anomalies from platform anomalies — the degradation originates at the transport layer, not compute.
15. eCPRI Frame Loss Pattern: Packet loss on the fronthaul port follows a burst pattern rather than random noise, consistent with switch congestion from competing traffic. The loss rate is correlated with FH link utilization spikes rather than with SINR or CPU metrics
